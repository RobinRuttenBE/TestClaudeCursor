# Baby Balloon Column with Tulle and Foil Topper

> **Category:** Column design, baby/ceremony decor, tulle work, frame covering
> **Products used:** Sempertex 360s (white — for frame covering), Sempertex 12" latex (pink — sized to 7" for base clusters), Sempertex 5" latex (pink — sized to 4" and 3" for top and accent clusters), Sempertex 260s (uninflated — for tying and securing), tulle (white + pink, 30cm width), Sempertex foil balloon (moon with sleeping bear), silk flowers, column stand with pole, aluminium/steel rods (for T-frame)

## Overview

A complete baby-themed column design featuring a custom T-shaped frame covered with 360 spirals, tulle draping in alternating colors, balloon clusters at base and top, a tulle bow at the midpoint, silk flower accents, and a foil character topper. This is a ceremony-style column suitable for baby showers, christenings, communions, and weddings.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Elegant ceremony piece with high perceived value
- Tulle adds luxury look at minimal material cost
- Modular design — adaptable to any theme by changing colors and topper
- Multiple revenue streams: hire or sell, with upsell on flowers and topper
- Teaches frame-building, covering, and decorating in one design

## Building the Frame

### Materials
- Column stand with center pole
- 2x aluminium or steel rods

### Steps

1. **Bend each rod into a right angle** — creating an L-shape.
2. **Insert both rods into the top of the pole** — positioned to form a T-shape (one extending left, one extending right).
3. The T-frame creates two horizontal arms for tulle draping.

---

## Covering the Frame with 360s

Uses the same spiral-wrap technique (see: `covering-base-pole-360.md`).

### Steps

1. **Inflate a small bubble** on a Sempertex 360 (white).
2. **Start at the top or bottom** of the frame.
3. **Wrap the bubble around the pole**, moving in one direction, covering the entire frame surface.
4. **When you reach the end:** cut excess balloon, or if possible, make a small hole to let remaining air out gradually while continuing the wrap.
5. **If one 360 isn't enough:** tie off, start a new one, and continue from where you stopped.
6. **Repeat for the T-arms** — cover all visible metal/mechanics.

**Purpose:** Hides all structural mechanics. Visible frames look unprofessional.

## Base Clusters

### Bottom base
- **2x Sempertex 12" latex** (pink, inflated to 7") — create a duplet and place directly on the stand base.
- Covers the black base and provides a foundation.

### Top base accent
- **4x Sempertex 5" latex** (pink, inflated to 4") — cluster of four, placed over the 12" duplet.
- Covers mechanics visible between the larger balloons.

---

## Top Clusters

### First cluster (at T-junction)
- **4x Sempertex 5" latex** (pink, inflated to 4") — position so the two T-frame arms pass exactly through the middle of the cluster.

### Second cluster (stabilizer)
- **4x Sempertex 5" latex** (pink, inflated to 3") — smaller, pushed up against the first cluster.
- Adds stability and ensures the top cluster sits straight.
- The frame arms must pass through the center of both clusters.

## Adding Tulle

### Measuring and cutting

1. **Measure from the top of the frame to the floor.**
2. **Double that length** — tulle is folded double because it's semi-transparent. Single layer looks thin.
3. **Cut three pieces per side** of the T-frame (6 total).

### Color sequence
Alternate colors: **white → pink → white** on each side.

### Attaching tulle

1. **Drape the doubled tulle over the T-frame arm** so it hangs evenly on both sides.
2. **Start with the tulle closest to the balloons** (white), then pink in the middle, then white on the outside.
3. **At the cluster level:** pull the tulle slightly and wrap once around the cluster to secure. One wrap is sufficient.
4. **Hide excess tulle ends** by tucking them under the balloon clusters.
5. **Open/fluff the tulle** after securing — can be done at the end, not critical to do immediately.

---

## Midpoint Tulle Bow

### Steps

1. **Tie a piece of uninflated 260** around the column at your desired bow height (eyeball it — or measure for consistency).
2. **Cut approximately 3 meters of tulle.**
3. **Fold into segments** — as many as possible from the length (same technique as `tulle-bow.md`).
4. **Tie in the middle** with the 260, as tight as possible.
5. **Cut excess 260.**
6. **Open all segments** to create a full bow.

The bow hides the mechanics at the attachment point and adds an elegant accent.

---

## Silk Flower Accents

### Steps

1. **Tie two silk flowers together** using a piece of uninflated 260.
2. **Use the 260 tail to wrap around a cluster** and tie in position.
3. **Find the best position** — typically at the base clusters where they add elegance without extra work.

**Tip:** Silk flowers provide immediate elegance with minimal effort. Always factor them into your pricing.

---

## Foil Topper Attachment

### The loop method (Alberto's technique)

This is different from the standard wrapping method:

1. **Take a piece of 260** (uninflated).
2. **Create a loop** with the 260.
3. **Pass the foil balloon's valve through the loop.**
4. **Tie both the valve and the 260 together** — this locks the foil to the 260.
5. **Do this twice** for extra security.
6. **Wrap the 260 around the top clusters** to attach the foil topper to the column.

### Top tulle accent

Add a smaller tulle bow at the very top (between the topper and top clusters):
- Same technique as the midpoint bow, but with less tulle (less than 3m).
- Hides the attachment mechanics of the topper.

## Tips

- Always double your tulle — single layer looks cheap and transparent
- Hide all mechanics (260 tails, knots, frame joints) under balloons or tulle
- The 360 frame covering is essential — never leave raw metal visible
- Tulle can be pre-cut to length before arriving on-site
- Silk flowers are reusable across events if you hire the design
- The loop attachment method for foils is more secure than simple wrapping

| Term | Meaning |
