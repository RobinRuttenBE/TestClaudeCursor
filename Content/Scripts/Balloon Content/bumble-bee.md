# Bumble Bee (Balloon Twisting Figure)

> **Source:** Tutorial video — Billy Bailey (Blue Magic Magazine)
> **Difficulty:** Intermediate
> **Category:** Balloon twisting, character/animal figure, entertainment decor
> **Products used:** Sempertex 260 (Goldenrod/yellow), Sempertex 260 (black), Sempertex 5" round (white — eyes), Sempertex 160 (black — antennae), Sempertex 160 (clear — wings)
> **Video reference:** Bumble bee

---

## Overview

A detailed bumble bee figure built from five balloons using a combination of twisting techniques: bubble making, lock twists, pinch twists, and fold twists. The design features a recognisable bee with antennae, eyes, a striped yellow-and-black body, a stinger, and clear wings. A popular figure for public events, bouquets, and children's parties that can be scaled down using 160s instead of 260s for a miniature version.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- One of the most requested children's party figures
- Uses multiple core twisting techniques in one design — excellent for skill development
- Works as a standalone figure, bouquet accent, flower arrangement addition, or stick topper
- Scalable: 260 version for standard size, 160 version for miniature/cute
- Five balloons total — moderate material use for high visual impact

## Step 1: Antennae (160 Black)

1. **Inflate a small puff** at one end of the 160 black — just a tiny amount of air.
2. **Stretch out the uninflated tail** to create the antenna stalk.
3. **Create two small antenna tips** using the squeeze-inflate technique (same as making a poodle tail — squeeze the inflated bubble to push air into the uninflated section while twisting).
4. The antennae should be thin stalks with small rounded tips.

---

## Step 2: Eyes (5" White Round)

1. **Inflate the 5" round to approximately baseball size.**
2. **Tie the knot tight and close to the balloon body** — you'll need the nozzle/neck area clear.
3. **Find the middle** of the round balloon.
4. **Split it in half** by twisting at the midpoint — creating two equal eye bubbles.
5. **Ensure the knot/nozzle is centred** between the two halves — if it's off-centre, one eye will be larger than the other, or worse, the nozzle protrudes like a growth.

> Getting the knot centred is the key detail. An off-centre split creates asymmetrical eyes that look wrong.

---

## Step 3: Head Assembly

1. **Position the antennae at the top.**
2. **Place the eyes (split 5" round) below the antennae.**
3. **Take the Goldenrod/yellow 260** and hold it right between the antennae and the eyeballs.
4. **Wrap to create the head structure** — the yellow 260 locks the antennae and eyes together into a head unit.

---

## Step 4: Body Stripes (260 Black + 260 Yellow)

The body is created by alternating yellow and black bubble segments.

### Building the striped body

1. **Start with the black 260** — tie it tightly at the head junction. The lips of the knot should be tucked inside/hidden.
2. **Make bubbles on the black 260** approximately the same size as the eye bubbles — consistency creates the striped pattern.
3. **Create pairs of black bubbles** and fold them in half (lock twist) — this creates a segment that sits across the body.
4. **Push the yellow 260 through the centre** of each pair of black bubbles — this locks the segments together and continues the body.
5. **Alternate:** yellow segment visible, black segment wraps around, yellow visible, black wraps around.
6. **The pressure from the lock twists** makes the bubbles push outward, creating the round body segments characteristic of a bee.

### Continuing the pattern

- Repeat the bubble-pair-and-push-through technique for 2–3 body segments.
- If segments look slightly wonky, spin them around to even them out.
- The same technique (alternating colours pushed through centre) can be extended for longer bodies (raccoon tails, caterpillars, etc.).

---

## Step 5: Stinger

1. **When the body is the desired length**, stop adding segments.
2. **Clip/cut the excess black 260.**
3. **On the yellow 260**, make a small twist/bubble near the end of the body.
4. **Leave a small pointed tail** — this becomes the stinger.
5. **Tie off** the end.

---

## Step 6: Wings (160 Clear)

1. **Inflate a 160 clear balloon.**
2. **Fold it in half** to create a loop — this is one wing pair.
3. **Make a second loop** of approximately the same size at the centre point — creating a figure-of-eight / two wing shapes.
4. **Thread the wing assembly through the centre of the body** — push it between the body segments at the appropriate position (typically between the first and second body segment).
5. **Cut any excess 160** to clean up the design.
6. **Shape the wings** — adjust the loops so they sit at the correct angle (slightly angled back, like real bee wings).

---

## Step 7: Face Details

- **Draw a smile or expression** using a marker — a big smile for friendly, a smirk for character.
- Facial expression dramatically changes the personality of the bee.
- Can also add drawn-on eye pupils to the white 5" round.

## Design Variations

| Variation | How |
|---|---|
| Miniature bee | Use all 160s instead of 260s — creates a small, cute version ideal for bouquet accents |
| Bouquet bee | Attach to a stick and insert into a flower bouquet or balloon bouquet |
| Flower accent | Sit the bee on top of a balloon flower as if pollinating |
| Scary bee / hornet | Draw an angry expression instead of a smile — works for Halloween |
| Colour variations | Swap Goldenrod for other yellows, or create fantasy bees in non-traditional colours |
| Wasp | Slimmer body (fewer segments), more pointed stinger, clear or light-coloured wings |

## Tips

- **Centre the knot when splitting the 5" round for eyes** — off-centre splits are the most common mistake and immediately look wrong
- **Tie the black 260 tight at the head** — visible knot lips look messy; tuck them in
- **Keep bubble sizes consistent** for body segments — uneven segments break the stripe pattern
- **Push 260s through the centre firmly** — this is what creates the rounded, puffy segment look
- **Clear 160s for wings** — coloured wings work but clear is the most realistic and professional
- **Make on 160s for a mini version** — small bees are disproportionately popular with children
- **Add to bouquets and flowers** — the bee is one of the few twisting figures that naturally complements floral designs

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Lock twist | Two bubbles folded together and twisted at the base to lock — creates body segments |
| Squeeze-inflate | Squeezing an inflated bubble to push air into an uninflated section — used for antennae tips |
| Split | Twisting a round balloon at its midpoint to create two equal halves — used for eyes |
| Segment | One striped section of the bee's body, created by alternating colour bubbles with lock twists |
| 260 | A long, thin modelling balloon (2" diameter, 60" length) — used for the body |
| 160 | A thinner modelling balloon (1" diameter, 60" length) — used for antennae and wings |
| 5" round | A small round latex balloon — used for eyes in this design |
| Goldenrod | A warm yellow colour in the Sempertex range — the standard "bee yellow" |
| Stinger | A small pointed tail at the end of the bee's body |
| Figure-of-eight | Two loops created from one balloon — used for the wing shape |
