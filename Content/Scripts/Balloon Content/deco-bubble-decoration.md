## Overview

A complete tabletop deco bubble display with 260 detailing around the edge (both smooth and pearl-string styles), 260 flower accents, a balloon base cluster, and a water weight. This transforms a standard stuffed deco bubble into a premium, detailed centerpiece. The technique covers the full build from base to finishing touches.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Tabletop centerpieces are high-demand, high-margin products
- Scalable to any budget — can be simple or elaborate
- Every added element (pearls, flowers, foliage) increases the price point
- Perfect for birthdays, Mother's Day, weddings, and corporate events
- Teaches multiple sub-techniques in one design

## Technique 1: Adding a 260 Around the Bubble Edge (Smooth Style)

### Steps

1. **Inflate a 260** with approximately 6 pumps.

2. **Measure against the bubble.** Wrap around to check if it covers half the circumference. Doesn't need to be exactly half — slightly off-center is fine.

3. **Tie off the 260.** If you're less confident, tie it first then attach. More confident? Tie directly onto the bubble.

4. **Attach at the base.** Find a couple of balloon necks at the bottom of the bubble and tie the 260 in underneath — hide all mechanics beneath the bubble.

5. **Follow the seam** of the deco bubble as a guide line for placement.

6. **Glue at three points:** start, middle, and top. Use U-Glue adhesive squares.

7. **Tie off at the top** and trim excess.

---

## Technique 2: Adding a Pearl String Around the Bubble Edge

### Steps

1. **Inflate a 260** with approximately 2 pumps.

2. **Attach at the base** — wrap around a neck and tie, hiding mechanics underneath.

3. **Create pearl bubbles.** Use two fingers as a size guide, twist 8 times per pearl to prevent unraveling.

4. **Work your way up the bubble**, gluing pearls into position along the seam with U-Glue squares.

5. **If you run out of length:** tie off, make a new pearl string, connect it to the last pearl, and continue. The join is invisible once glued.

6. **Secure the last pearl** with a glue dot underneath the bubble and trim excess.

## U-Glue Tips (Important)

- **Always warm up U-Glue squares** before use — rub between your hands. Cold adhesive squares don't stick properly.
- **Test:** If the white backing paper doesn't peel off easily, the square isn't warm enough.
- **Latex to latex or latex to plastic:** One square is sufficient.
- **Foil to foil:** Use two squares — one on each surface, then press the two adhesive sides together. Single squares don't hold foil-to-foil reliably.
- **Don't force position.** Place where the balloon naturally touches — forcing creates pressure that can pop the balloon.

## Adding 260 Flower Accents

- Pre-made 260 flowers (woven spiral flower, open flower styles) are attached as decorative accents.
- **Preferred method:** Tie into the design first for security, then glue into final position.
- **Backup method:** Latex-to-latex with glue dots works well, but tying is more reliable.
- Flowers can be placed around the base, at the sides, or trailing up the bubble.
- Leave connection points on your flowers during construction for easy attachment later.

## Building the Base

### Cluster structure
- **Main clusters:** Sempertex 12" latex (Tropical Coral) sized to 7" — three clusters grouped together
- **Filler clusters:** Small balloons with 1-2 pumps, added between main clusters for fullness
- **Side accents:** Small balloons with 2 pumps to add thickness and shape

### Water weight tip
- Use a water weight at the bottom for stability.
- **Always use a contrasting bright color** that's completely different from the design palette.
- Reason: so the client immediately recognizes it's a weight and doesn't accidentally remove it.
- Always tell the client the water weight is there.
- Exception: if the weight is visible through the display, match it to the design color instead.

## Scaling to Client Budget

| Budget level | What to include |
|---|---|
| **Basic** | Stuffed bubble + simple base cluster + water weight |
| **Standard** | Add 260 edge detail (smooth or pearls) + base filler |
| **Premium** | Add 260 flowers + larger base + additional detailing |
| **Luxury** | Add helium elements on top + more 260 flowers trailing up sides + bigger base + extra organic accents |

The design is modular — each added element justifies a higher price point.

---

## Tips

- Always prep elements in advance (flowers, pearl strings, base clusters) — assembly on-site should be combining, not creating
- Hide all mechanics (knots, ties, connections) underneath the bubble
- Follow the deco bubble's seam as your guide line for straight placement
- Cut excess material as you go for a clean finish
- Don't pop the bubble by applying too much pressure when gluing — place where natural contact occurs

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Deco Bubble | A clear, round, stretchy plastic balloon — similar to a Bobo but with different material properties |
| Bubble hug | A garland of small balloons wrapped around the equator of a deco bubble or Bobo |
| Pearl string | A series of small, equally-sized bubbles made from a 260, resembling a string of pearls |
| U-Glue | Adhesive squares/dots used for attaching balloons and decorative elements to each other |
| Water weight | A water-filled balloon used as a base weight to prevent displays from tipping over |
| 260 flowers | Decorative flower shapes made from modelling balloons (various styles — spiral, open, rosette) |
| Duplet | Two balloons tied together — combined into clusters for the base |
