## Sempertex Colombia (The Factory)

- **Location:** Barranquilla, on Colombia's Caribbean coast — strategically positioned with access to two shipping ports.
- **Founded:** 1938 by Emil Louie (grandfather of current CEO Leo).
- **Still a family business** — the family values are felt in how they do business.
- **Workforce:** ~1,300 employees. 70% women. The company runs a social program for women.
- **Scale:** The factory is approximately one kilometre long. Operates 24-hour shifts (day and night).
- **Growth:** Recently opened a second factory adjacent to the first — a "copy-paste" expansion due to rapid growth.
- **Export reach:** 70 countries worldwide.
- **Responsiveness:** The factory listens to market feedback — they've reintroduced colours and references based on distributor input. Being a family company makes them accessible and open to suggestions.

## Sempertex Europe (STX EU)

- **Location:** Brussels, Belgium.
- **Territory:** Full European distribution + UK (UK added recently — described as "a present").
- **Origin story:** Sofie and Kun started as balloon artists/twisters. Sofie was working with another brand (one that no longer exists — implied Qualatex). She discovered Sempertex balloons and fell in love with the quality, particularly for twisting work — more colours in 260s and 160s than the competition at the time.
- **Growth path:** Suitcases → cartons → larger orders → contacted by Colombia to open a European distributorship. Sofie said yes without hesitation.
- **Started by selling to friends** in the balloon community — it was a hobby that became a business.
- **Timeline:** Approaching 15 years (as of the interview).
- **Recent growth:** Bought a second building (primarily warehousing). Office space is tight — further expansion likely.
- **Greg's description of Sofie:** "A spreadsheet person" — likes everything to be organised and precise. This has worked well for wholesaler relationships.

## Why Buy Sempertex Balloons (Quality Differentiators)

Sofie's key quality indicators — these are the official selling points from the CEO:

| Quality factor | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Elasticity** | Stretchy, flexible latex — especially important for the neck. Good elasticity = easier tying and twisting |
| **Consistent latex distribution** | The latex is evenly spread throughout the balloon — no thin spots, no thick spots. Hold a balloon to the light: no streaks, no dots |
| **Low pop rate** | Sempertex balloons rarely pop. Very few returns or complaints. ("Balloons do pop sometimes — every brand. But we get hardly any complaints.") |
| **Colour consistency** | A red balloon bought today is the same red in 3 months. Twisting balloons and round balloons in the same colour match each other |
| **Cross-reference colour matching** | You can mix different balloon sizes and types (260s, 5", 12", etc.) and the colours match across all references |

> Sofie's advice to newcomers: "I think you should figure out yourself which brand you like." — She doesn't hard-sell. She lets the quality speak and identifies the key indicators people should evaluate.

## Best-Selling Colours

### Top performers (as of interview)
1. **Reflex Gold** — the overall winner, in both 12" and 5"
2. **White Sand** — consistently strong performer
3. **Traditional colours:** White, Black, Red (in 5" and 12")
4. **Eucalyptus** — still performing well years after launch
5. **Puzzle Dust Cream** — new colour that performed immediately because it matches well with many other colours

### New colour launches (recent)
- Aurora Green
- Green Tea — performed well
- Rose — **top performer among new colours**
- Periwinkle
- Gold Dust
- Midnight Gray

### Colour launch insight
> "Sometimes you launch a color and you think this is great and then the market doesn't." — Sofie acknowledges the unpredictability of colour reception. New colours for 2026 are planned but not revealed; Sofie expressed excitement about an early-2026 launch.

## UK vs European Market Differences

| Aspect | UK | Mainland Europe |
|---|---|---|
| **Dominant style** | Organic — "really really really big" | More balanced — organic + classic/traditional |
| **Twisting** | Less twisting | More twisting, especially Northern Europe |
| **Market split** | ~80% decor | More varied |
| **Professionalism** | Very high — wholesalers, distributors, decorators all very professional | High, but UK stands out |
| **Colour preferences** | Imperial Red sells much more in the UK than in mainland Europe | Varies by country |
| **Trend adoption** | Classic decor coming back; many blending organic + classic | Classic never fully left |

> "The level of professionalism that we see in decorators but also in wholesalers and distributors is really... very very good." — Sofie on the UK market.
