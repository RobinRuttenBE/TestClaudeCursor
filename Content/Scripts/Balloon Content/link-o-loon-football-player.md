# Link-o-Loon Football Player

> **Source:** Sempertex Europe tutorial video — Alberto Falcone (Brand Ambassador)
> **Difficulty:** Advanced
> **Category:** Link-o-Loon construction, character design, hanging/ceiling decor, event theming
> **Products used:** Sempertex Link-o-Loon (multiple colours, inflated to various sizes), Sempertex 5" latex (black + Merlot + Malibu Pitch, inflated to 4–5"), Sempertex 12" latex (inflated to 8"), Sempertex 24" latex (Malibu Pitch — head), Sempertex 260s (green — grass detail), monofilament fishing line (0.7mm)
> **Video reference:** Link-o-Loon football player

---

## Overview

A full-body football player figure built entirely from Link-o-Loon chains and balloon clusters. The design uses two curved limb sections (legs and arms) connected at the torso with monofilament line, topped with a large round head. The curve in each limb is created by using two different chain sizes side by side — the size differential naturally bends the structure. Can be hung from a ceiling or placed on a base.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- High-impact character design for sports events, parties, and venue decor
- Fully customisable — change colours for any team, any sport
- Can be hung (no base needed) or floor-standing with a grass base
- Demonstrates advanced Link-o-Loon technique that sets professionals apart
- Reusable concept — same construction method works for any humanoid character

## Core Concept: How the Curve Works

The key engineering principle behind this design: **when you attach two Link-o-Loon chains of different sizes side by side, the size difference creates a natural curve.** The larger chain forms the outside of the curve, the smaller chain forms the inside. Greater size difference = more pronounced curve.

This single concept is what makes the entire football player possible.

---

## The Legs (Bottom Section)

### Chain construction

Each leg requires **4 chains** total:

| Chain | Balloon type | Inflated to | Quantity per leg | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Large chain | Link-o-Loon | 9" | 2 | Outside of curve (7 balloons per chain) |
| Small chain | Link-o-Loon | 6" | 2 | Inside of curve (7 balloons per chain) |

### Colour sequence
Both large and small chains follow the **same colour sequence** — this creates the visual sections of the leg:
- Shoes (bottom section) — one colour
- Socks (middle section) — different colour
- Legs/skin — different colour
- Shorts (top section) — different colour

Change colours within the chain to define each body part.

### Square spacers

Between the chains, square-shaped spacers fill the gaps:

| Spacer type | Balloon | Inflated to | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Link-o-Loon squares | 4x black Link-o-Loon | 6" | Primary spacers |
| 5" round squares | 4x black 5" | 4" | Alternating spacers |

Each chain end is finished with a **5" black balloon inflated to 4"**.

### Assembly

1. **Take one square spacer** and tie to the first position using the 5" end balloon and the first Link-o-Loon in the chain.
2. **Place the small (6") chain directly next to the large (9") chain** — not diagonally, but parallel.
3. **Work space by space** — do not skip any gaps. Wrap each spacer around systematically.
4. **Continue around all chains** with alternating spacers until the full leg section is assembled.
5. The size differential (9" vs 6") naturally curves the structure into a leg shape.

---

## The Arms (Top Section)

### Same concept, different proportions

The arms use the identical construction method but with adjusted sizes:

| Difference | Legs | Arms |
|---|---|---|
| Balloons per chain | 7 | 5 |
| Large chain size | 9" | 9" |
| Small chain size | 6" | 8" |
| Curve intensity | More pronounced (3" difference) | Subtler (1" difference) |
| Square spacers | Same sizes as legs | Same sizes as legs |

The smaller size difference (9" vs 8" instead of 9" vs 6") creates a gentler curve — appropriate for arms vs legs.

---

## Connecting Legs and Arms Together

### Building the torso clusters

1. **Leg torso cluster:** Make two 4-balloon clusters using Sempertex 5" inflated to 4" (in the shorts colour). Tie them together **not as a standard cross-head cluster, but stacked one over the other** — like a small cube. Place in the centre of the leg section.
2. **Arm torso cluster:** Same technique — two 4-balloon clusters using 5" inflated to 4" (in the shirt colour, e.g. Merlot). Stack as a cube. Place in the centre of the arm section.

### Joining with monofilament

1. **Use 0.7mm monofilament fishing line** — must be this thickness or similar. Too thin = unstable.
2. **Start at the leg cluster** — wrap the fishing line around the cube cluster and tie securely. It must not slide.
3. **Pass the fishing line through the arm section** — thread through the arm cube cluster.
4. **Pull tight** — bring arms and legs as close together as possible.
5. **Wrap and tie** the fishing line around the arm clusters to lock everything in place.

> This connection method also makes the design suitable for hanging from a ceiling.

## Adding Stability

The gap between arms and legs/torso needs filling for both structural stability and visual completeness.

### Filler balloons

| Position | Balloon | Inflated to | Quantity | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Between arms (2 gaps) | 12" latex | 8" | 2 duplets (4 balloons total) | One duplet per gap, passed through the structure |
| Between legs (2 gaps) | 12" latex | 8" | 2 duplets + 1 single (5 balloons total) | Two duplets for the larger gaps, one single for the remaining hole |

These filler balloons serve a dual purpose: they fill the empty spaces and push the limbs into the correct position, giving the figure its proper form.

## The Head

### Inflating

1. **Use a Sempertex 24" latex** (Malibu Pitch or skin-tone colour).
2. **Over-inflate first, then deflate** until the balloon is perfectly round and quite soft. The exact size doesn't matter — the round shape does.

### Building the neck base

1. **First cluster:** 4x Sempertex 5" inflated to 4" — tie as tight as possible to the centre top of the body using fishing line.
2. **Second cluster:** 4x Sempertex 5" in Malibu Pitch, inflated to 5" — tie tightly on top of the first cluster.
3. **Adjust both clusters** to create a stable, flat platform for the head.
4. **Fill any visible gaps** with duplets made from 5" balloons inflated to 4", tied with a long gap between them so they can straddle and cover holes.

### Attaching the head

1. **Tie a knot around the fishing line** at the neck position.
2. **Slide the fishing line through the head balloon's knot** and pull tight simultaneously.
3. **Tie as tight as possible** — the head must sit firmly on the neck base.
4. **Cut excess fishing line.**

## Display Options

### Option 1: Hanging (no base)
- The monofilament construction makes this design ideal for hanging from ceilings.
- Works well in large venues — visible from a distance.
- No additional materials needed.

### Option 2: Floor-standing with grass base
- Build a base and cover it — the base is a mechanical/structural element that should be hidden.
- **Grass cover:** 2 simple clusters using 12" and 5" green balloons + 4x green Sempertex 260s arranged to look like grass blades.
- The grass disguise turns a functional base into a design feature.

## Tips

- **The curve comes from size difference** — larger difference = more curve. This is the fundamental principle
- **Work space by space** with the square spacers — skipping gaps creates structural weakness
- **Use 0.7mm monofilament** — thinner line won't hold; thicker is harder to work with
- **Over-inflate then deflate the head** — this is the only way to guarantee a round shape
- **Stack torso clusters as cubes, not cross-heads** — this creates a more compact, stable connection point
- **Filler balloons do structural work** — they're not just decorative, they hold the form together
- **Cover your base** — never leave mechanical parts visible. The grass detail is simple but essential
- **Colour changes within chains define body parts** — plan your colour sequence before you start inflating

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Link-o-Loon | A latex balloon with a tail on both ends, designed to link together into chains and structures |
| Chain | A series of Link-o-Loons connected end-to-end |
| Square spacer | A group of 4 balloons (Link-o-Loon or round) used to fill gaps between parallel chains |
| Monofilament | Transparent fishing line used for structural connections — 0.7mm recommended |
| Cube cluster | Two 4-balloon clusters stacked one on top of the other (not crossed) — creates a compact connection point |
| Duplet | Two balloons tied together at the neck |
| Cross-head | Standard cluster arrangement where two duplets are crossed at 90° — NOT used for the torso in this design |
| Filler balloon | A larger balloon (typically 12" inflated to 8") used to fill structural gaps and stabilise the form |
| Colour sequence | The planned order of colours within a chain that defines different body parts (shoes, socks, legs, shorts) |
| Size differential curve | The engineering principle: two parallel chains of different sizes create a natural bend |
