> **Products used:** Sempertex latex balloons (any size), balloon stick (optional, for demonstration)

## Overview

The duplet is the single most important building block in balloon decoration. From duplets you make clusters (quads), and from clusters you build garlands, columns, arches, and nearly every professional balloon structure. This tutorial covers both how to tie a proper duplet and — crucially — how to undo one without wasting balloons when you've made a mistake in size or color.

**Why this matters:**
- A properly tied duplet is the foundation of all decor work
- Knowing how to undo one saves balloons, time, and money
- Reduces waste — especially important with premium colors or large orders

---

## Technique: Creating the Perfect Duplet

### Steps

1. **Inflate two balloons to the desired size** (same size for standard work, but different sizes are fine for specific designs).

2. **Pull the two necks well apart.** Stretch them out so you have enough length to work with.

3. **Wrap the necks around each other twice.** Two wraps gives enough grip to hold securely.

4. **Tie a single knot — but don't overtighten.** A normal knot is absolutely sufficient. An overly tight knot makes the duplet impossible to undo later if you need to correct a mistake.

### Key principle
The knot does not need to be extremely tight. Two wraps + a standard knot = secure enough for any professional structure. Overtightening only creates problems.

---

## Technique: Undoing a Duplet Knot

### When to use
- Wrong balloon size in the duplet
- Wrong color selected
- Need to reuse a balloon for a different purpose
- Adjusting a cluster after assembly

### Steps

1. **Pull the two necks apart** to create some slack in the knot area.

2. **Locate the small loop** that forms between the two wraps when you pull the necks. This loop is the key.

3. **Insert a stick (or your finger) through the loop.**

4. **Pull through the loop** — the knot unravels cleanly.

5. **Result:** Both balloons are separated, undamaged, and can be reinflated or reused.

### Why it works
Because you only tied a single (not overtight) knot after two wraps, the structure unravels in reverse when you pull through the loop. If you had tied multiple tight knots, this wouldn't be possible.

---

## Tips

- Always wrap twice, knot once — this is the universal best practice
- Use a balloon stick or thin rod to help find and pull through the loop, especially if your fingers are too large
- This technique works on any balloon size
- Practice on a few test duplets before doing it on a live project
- The ability to undo duplets means you waste fewer balloons — significant cost savings over time on large jobs

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Duplet | Two balloons tied together at the neck — the fundamental building block of balloon decor |
| Cluster / Quad | Four balloons (two duplets) twisted together — the standard unit for garlands and columns |
| Neck | The narrow uninflated section at the base of an inflated balloon, used for tying |
| Wrap | Winding the necks of two balloons around each other before knotting |
| Garland | A long string of connected clusters forming an organic or structured balloon display |
