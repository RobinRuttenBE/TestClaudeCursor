# Mastering the Raisin Twist

> **Source:** Sempertex Europe tutorial video — Alberto Falcone (Brand Ambassador)
> **Difficulty:** Beginner–Intermediate
> **Category:** Twisting technique, modelling fundamentals, attachment method
> **Products used:** Sempertex latex balloons (any round size), Sempertex 260 (for tying), small round object (pearl, bean, plastic sphere, or knotted 260 piece)
> **Video reference:** Master the raisin twist

---

## Overview

The raisin twist creates an attachment point anywhere on the surface of a round balloon — not just at the neck. This allows you to connect balloons, accessories, or structural elements to any spot on a balloon's surface. Alberto teaches two methods: the traditional (inside) method and the outside method, each with different advantages.

**Why this matters:**
- Creates attachment points anywhere on a balloon — not limited to the neck
- Essential for character designs, sculptures, and complex structures
- Two methods for different situations — inside for pre-planned, outside for precise positioning
- Foundational technique used across many advanced designs

---

## Method 1: Traditional Raisin Twist (Inside)

The object is placed inside the balloon before inflation.

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex latex balloon (uninflated)
- 1x small round object (pearl, bean, plastic sphere, or a cluster of 260 knots)
- 1x piece of Sempertex 260 (uninflated, for tying)

### Making a "raisin" from 260 scraps
If you don't have beads or beans, tie several knots on top of each other from a piece of 260 to create a small, round, hard shape. This works just as well as any other object.

### Steps

1. **Insert the round object** into the uninflated balloon. Position it where you want the attachment point (e.g., directly opposite the neck).

2. **Pinch the object from outside.** Grip the balloon surface around the object so it's isolated in a small pinch of latex.

3. **Wrap with 260.** Take a piece of uninflated 260 and wrap it around the pinched area several times. Pull tight to make it very secure.

4. **Tie with a double knot.** As tight as possible — this needs to hold permanently.

5. **Inflate the balloon.** You now have two attachment points: the natural neck and the raisin twist.

### Best for
- Pre-planned designs where you know the attachment point location in advance
- Creating a secondary attachment point directly opposite the neck

## Method 2: Outside Raisin Twist

The object is placed on the outside of an already-inflated balloon.

### Steps

1. **Inflate the balloon and tie it off normally.** The balloon must be **soft** (underinflated) — you need slack in the latex to pinch.

2. **Pinch the balloon surface** at the exact point where you want the attachment. Pull the latex outward like pinching skin.

3. **Place the round object (pearl, bean, etc.) under the pinch** — between the pinched latex and the balloon surface.

4. **Pinch again over the object,** trapping it in the fold of latex.

5. **Twist a couple of times** to lock the object in place.

6. **Secure with 260.** Wrap and tie tightly with a piece of uninflated 260.

### Best for
- Precise positioning — you can see exactly where the attachment point will be
- Adjustments on already-inflated balloons
- Situations where the attachment point location depends on the surrounding design

### Pro tip
**Use a marker** to make a small dot on the balloon surface before pinching. This ensures the raisin twist ends up in exactly the right spot — especially important for character faces or symmetrical designs.

## Comparison

| | Inside method | Outside method |
|---|---|---|
| **When to place** | Before inflation | After inflation |
| **Precision** | Approximate (can't see exact position) | Very precise (can see and mark position) |
| **Balloon state** | Uninflated | Must be soft/underinflated |
| **Best for** | Pre-planned attachment points | Precise positioning, adjustments |
| **Difficulty** | Easier | Slightly harder (requires finesse) |

## Applications

- **Character designs:** Eyes, noses, ears, or limbs attached to round body parts
- **Sculptures:** Connecting structural elements at non-standard angles
- **Floral designs:** Attaching stems or leaves to round balloon "buds"
- **Displays:** Connecting accent pieces to large round feature balloons
- **Any design** requiring an attachment point that isn't at the balloon's neck

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Raisin twist | A technique that creates an attachment point on the surface of a round balloon by trapping a small object in a pinch of latex |
| Pinch | Gripping the balloon surface between fingers to isolate a small section of latex |
| Attachment point | Any place on a balloon where another element can be tied, wrapped, or connected |
| 260 | Sempertex modelling balloon, 2" x 60" — used here uninflated as tying material |
| Underinflated | A balloon inflated to less than full capacity, leaving the latex soft and pliable |
