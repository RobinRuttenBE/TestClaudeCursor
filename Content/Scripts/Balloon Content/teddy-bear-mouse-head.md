# How to Make a Teddy Bear with Mouse Head Balloons

> **Source:** Sempertex Europe tutorial video — Alberto Falcone (Brand Ambassador)
> **Difficulty:** Intermediate–Advanced
> **Category:** Character design, twisting, sculpting, gifting product
> **Products used:** Sempertex Mouse Head balloons (2x, various colors available), Sempertex 260s (black for eyes, matching color for tying, contrasting color for bow), Sempertex 360s (2x, cut in half, for arms), Sempertex 5" rounds (matching color for shoulders — 4-balloon cluster, plus 2 for securing bow), Sempertex 6" Link-O-Loon (cream or contrasting color for nose)
> **Video reference:** How to make a teddy bear with mouse head balloons

---

## Overview

A balloon teddy bear built from two Sempertex Mouse Head balloons — one for the head, one for the body. The mouse head shape provides natural "ears" that become the bear's ears (head) and the bear's legs (body). This is a versatile character design popular for both children and adults, suitable for gifts, party decorations, baby showers, and retail displays.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Universally appealing — teddy bears work across all age groups and occasions
- Uses a specialty balloon shape (Mouse Head) that most people haven't seen
- Impressive "wow factor" relative to the skill required
- Available in multiple colors — customisable to any theme
- Excellent retail/gift product with high perceived value

## The Head

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex Mouse Head balloon
- 1x Sempertex 260 (any color — will be hidden, used for tying)

### Steps

1. **Inflate the mouse head partially.** Inflate until you have a small round head with small ears. Make it softer than normal — overinflate slightly, then deflate to your desired size. The balloon needs to be soft because you'll be manipulating it.

2. **Create the muzzle area.** Pinch the surface at the center-front of the head to create a raisin twist. Use a piece of uninflated 260 to tie and secure the pinch. This creates the protruding muzzle where the nose will attach.

   **Tip:** You can also use a small bean or plastic bubble inside the pinch for a rounder muzzle shape.

---

## The Nose

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex 6" Link-O-Loon (cream or preferred nose color)

### Steps

1. **Inflate a very small bubble** from the Link-O-Loon — approximately 2 inches, no more.

2. **Tie the tip and neck together.** This creates a slightly squeezed oval shape rather than a perfect round — more nose-like.

---

## The Muzzle

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex 5" round (same color as the mouse head)

### Steps

1. **Stress the balloon first.** Fully inflate, then deflate, then re-inflate to approximately 3.5 inches. This makes the latex softer and easier to work with.

2. **Tie high on the neck.** Leave a longer neck than usual — don't tie right at the balloon.

3. **Attach the nose to the muzzle.** Tie the nose and muzzle neck together, leaving a small gap between them.

4. **Apple twist the muzzle.** Perform an apple twist and secure with another piece of uninflated 260.

5. **Position the nose.** Push one side of the muzzle slightly so the nose sits a bit higher — more natural-looking.

6. **Attach to the head.** Tie the muzzle assembly tightly to the raisin twist on the head.

---

## The Eyes

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex 260 (black)

### Steps

1. **Create two small pinch twists** from the same 260. Inflate a small section, make a pinch twist, deflate the remainder, then make a second pinch twist of the same size from the other end.

2. **Check both are equal size** before cutting and tying.

3. **Cut and separate.** You now have two individual pinch twist "eyes."

4. **Tie together with a gap.** Connect the two eyes with uninflated 260 between them — the gap determines how far apart the eyes sit on the face.

5. **Attach to the head.** Pass the connecting piece under the muzzle and over the top of the head. Important: **do not create tension** when attaching. If too tight, the eyes get pulled inward and distort the face.

---

## The Body

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex Mouse Head balloon

### Steps

1. **This is the hardest part.** You need to inflate the mouse head so that both "ears" expand fully to become the bear's legs.

2. **Inflate to the point where the main body is full**, then use your hands to push air into both ears evenly.

3. **Keep pushing and manipulating** until both ear sections are fully open and round. One ear often resists opening — keep working it with your hands.

4. **Tie directly to the head.** No need for a double knot — simply tie the body's neck to the head. This requires some hand strength and patience.

---

## The Shoulders

### Materials
- 4x Sempertex 5" rounds (matching color, inflated to ~3 inches)

### Steps

1. **Pre-make a 4-balloon cluster** from the 5" rounds.

2. **Position between head and body.** The cluster sits at the junction and serves dual purpose: it acts as shoulders and helps balance/stabilise the head-body connection.

---

## The Arms

### Materials
- 2x Sempertex 360s (cut in half — use the nozzle halves)

### Steps

1. **Cut each 360 approximately in half.** Use the nozzle end so you can inflate each piece separately.

2. **Inflate to arm-length.** Check proportions against the body — don't make them too long. Slightly shorter looks better.

3. **Curve them slightly** for a natural teddy bear arm pose.

4. **Tie both arms separately**, then tie them together with a gap between them (same technique as the eyes).

5. **Attach by passing under the shoulder cluster** and wrapping around one arm to secure.

---

## The Bow (Optional Finishing Touch)

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex 260 (contrasting color — e.g., honey yellow)

### Steps

1. **Inflate the 260** and create the following sequence:
   - One small bubble
   - Two loop twists (the bow loops)
   - One pinch twist in the middle (keeps the bow open)
   - One small bubble to finish

2. **Deflate the remaining 260.** Use the uninflated tail to tie the bow to the body by passing through the shoulder cluster.

3. **Secure on the back** using two of the 5" shoulder balloons to wrap around and hold the bow's tail in place.

## Key Techniques Used

| Technique | Where used | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Raisin twist | Muzzle area on head | Pinching the surface of a round balloon and twisting to create a protruding point |
| Apple twist | Muzzle attachment | Pushing the knot into the balloon and twisting to create a tucked, round shape |
| Pinch twist | Eyes, bow center | Inflating a small bubble and twisting the ends together to form a small round bead |
| Loop twist | Bow | Creating a loop from a section of 260 and twisting the base closed |
| Stress inflation | Muzzle (5" round) | Fully inflating then deflating a balloon before re-inflating to make it softer and more pliable |

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Mouse Head balloon | A Sempertex specialty shape balloon with two protruding "ear" sections |
| Raisin twist | Pinching and twisting the surface of a round balloon to create a small protruding nub |
| Apple twist | Pushing the knot of a balloon into the body and twisting to lock it in place |
| Pinch twist | A small inflated bubble twisted at both ends to form a bead shape |
| Loop twist | A section of modelling balloon formed into a loop and twisted at the base |
| Stress inflation | Over-inflating then deflating a balloon to soften the latex before final inflation |
| 4-balloon cluster | Four round balloons tied in pairs, then twisted together to form a flat group of four |
