# Woven Balloon Flowers

> **Source:** Tutorial video — Loretta (Perfectly Pop Balloons)
> **Difficulty:** Intermediate
> **Category:** Twisting / weaving, flower design, accent piece
> **Products used:** Sempertex 260s (3 colours for learning: Fashion White, Purple Orchid, Tropical Coral), hand pump or electric pump (with small nozzle)
> **Video reference:** Woven balloon flowers

---

## Overview

A woven (or spiral) flower made by interlocking three folded 260 balloons in a repeating over-under weaving pattern. The result is a symmetrical six-petal flower where each colour forms a visible figure-of-eight. Despite looking highly complex, the technique follows a simple three-step repeating sequence that becomes intuitive after a few attempts.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- High perceived complexity, low actual difficulty once the pattern clicks
- Versatile accent piece — works on bouquets, stacks, garlands, or standalone
- Scalable: nest a small (3x 260) version inside a large (6x 260) version for dramatic impact
- Minimal materials — only 3 balloons and a pump needed for the basic version
- Customisable centre — add a 260 flower, pollen-style accents, or leave open
- The "looks impossible but isn't" factor makes it ideal for tutorial content

## The Golden Tip: Learn with Three Colours

When learning this technique, **always use three different colours** (e.g. Fashion White, Purple Orchid, Tropical Coral). Using a single colour makes it nearly impossible to track the weaving pattern. Once you can do it confidently with three colours, switching to a block colour is straightforward.

This is the most important piece of advice for anyone attempting this design for the first time.

---

## Step 1: Creating Your Petals

### Inflating

1. **Stretch each 260** before inflating — this makes the latex more pliable for weaving.
2. **Inflate with 10 hand pumps** (or set your electric pump timer to equivalent).
3. **Before tying off: release a small amount of air.** The balloon should feel soft and squishy, not firm. Firm balloons will pop under weaving pressure.
4. **Tie off** the balloon.

### Folding into figure-of-eights

1. **Find the middle** by pulling the knotted end and the tail end apart.
2. **Push both ends together** so the balloon folds in half.
3. **Twist at the midpoint** — creating a figure-of-eight shape.
4. **Repeat for all three balloons.**

> This is the same folding technique used for balloon sprays.

---

## Step 2: Twist Them All Together

1. **Place all three figure-of-eights on your arm.**
2. **Pull apart and twist them together** at the central twist point — all six petals should now radiate from a shared centre.

---

## Step 3: Arranging Your Petals

1. **Lay the assembly flat on a table** — the table is your best tool for this step.
2. **Choose a starting colour** (e.g. Purple Orchid) and ensure each colour's two petals sit **opposite each other**.
3. **Arrange so all petals overlap in the same direction** — all should lay flat, facing the same way.
4. **Pick a direction** — clockwise or anticlockwise — and stay consistent throughout.

> Check: you should be able to put your hand through the centre and all petals pass through in the same direction.

---

## Step 4: Start the Weaving

This is the core technique. The pattern is a three-colour repeating sequence:

### The sequence (using Purple / White / Coral as example)

| Pull | Through |
|---|---|
| Purple | through White |
| White | through Coral |
| Coral | through Purple |
| Purple | through White |
| White | through Coral |
| Coral | through Purple |

That's **six pulls total** — each colour gets pulled through twice.

### How to execute

1. **Put your hand through the White petal** and pull the Coral up through it.
2. **Move to the next position** — pull the White through the Coral.
3. **Move to the next position** — pull the Coral through the Purple.
4. **Repeat the sequence** one more time (3 more pulls) for a total of 6.
5. **After all six pulls:** gently tug each petal upward to ensure the weave is even and all petals face the correct pattern.

---

## Step 5: The End Result

### How to verify your weave is correct

- **Front and back should be identical** — the flower looks the same from both sides.
- **Each colour should form a visible figure-of-eight** when traced individually.
- If any colour doesn't show a clear figure-of-eight, the weave has gone wrong somewhere.

### Adjusting tightness

| Want | Do |
|---|---|
| Tighter / more closed flower | Pull petals together at the back — the flower compresses inward |
| Looser / more open flower | Pull petals outward from the front — the flower opens up |

---

## Adding a Centre

The centre of the flower is open and can be filled with any accent:

- **260 flower:** Pre-make a small balloon flower from a 260 and thread it through the centre. Leave the uninflated tail of the 260 intact so you can tie it through the back to secure it.
- **Pollen-style accents:** Small bits protruding from the centre to mimic flower pollen.
- **Leave it open:** The woven pattern itself is already visually striking.

---

## Going Bigger: The Double Flower

1. **Make a second woven flower using 6x Sempertex 260s** — same inflation (10 pumps), same technique but with 6 balloons instead of 3.
2. **Place the smaller 3-balloon flower inside the larger 6-balloon flower** as a centre piece.
3. The result is a dramatically large, layered flower with exceptional visual impact.

## Tips

- **Always release air before tying** — soft balloons survive the weaving; firm ones pop
- **Three colours first, block colour later** — this is non-negotiable for learning
- **Work on a flat table** — trying to weave in the air is unnecessarily difficult
- **Stay consistent with your direction** — pick clockwise or anticlockwise and stick with it
- **Check for figure-of-eights** — each colour should trace a clear figure-of-eight when you look at the finished flower
- **The front and back must match** — if they don't, a pull went wrong
- **Leave the 260 tail on centre flowers** — it's your attachment mechanism

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 260 | A long, thin modelling balloon (2" diameter, 60" length when fully inflated) — the standard for twisting work |
| Figure-of-eight | The shape created when a 260 is folded in half and twisted at the midpoint — forms two loops |
| Weaving | The over-under interlocking technique that creates the woven flower pattern |
| Block colour | Using a single colour for all three balloons — looks cleaner but harder to learn |
| Petal | One loop of a folded 260 — each balloon creates two petals |
| Centre accent | Any decorative element placed in the open centre of the woven flower |
| Double flower | A small woven flower (3x 260) nested inside a larger one (6x 260) |
| Spray | A figure-of-eight balloon arrangement — uses the same folding technique as the woven flower petals |
