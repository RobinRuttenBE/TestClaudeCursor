## Overview

A classic balloon design made from a single 260 — one of Alberto's original designs that has become widely popular in the balloon community. It consists of 20 equally-sized bubbles twisted into a circle and then folded into a star shape. This is an excellent beginner exercise for learning consistent bubble sizing, which is a foundational skill for all balloon modelling.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Made from a single 260 — minimal material cost
- Great warm-up exercise and teaching tool

## Technique: 20-Bubble Star

### Materials
- 1x Sempertex 260

### Step 1: Inflate and make 20 bubbles

1. Fully inflate the 260, leaving a small tail.
2. Create **20 bubbles of equal size** along the length of the balloon. Count as you go.
3. Consistency is the challenge — this is what makes it a great practice exercise.

### Step 2: Form the circle

1. **Tie the first and last bubble together** using the neck/tail of the balloon.
2. Deflate any remaining uninflated section and tie off with a knot.
3. You now have a ring of 20 bubbles.

### Step 3: Twist into a star

1. Start from the knot.
2. **Count 3 bubbles**, then twist those 3 together at the base.
3. **Skip 1 bubble** (don't use it).
4. **Count 3 more bubbles**, twist together.
5. Repeat the pattern (twist 3, skip 1) all the way around the ring.
6. When you open it up, the twisted groups form the points of a star.

### Pattern summary
`[twist 3] — skip 1 — [twist 3] — skip 1 — [twist 3] — skip 1 — [twist 3] — skip 1 — [twist 3] — skip 1`

5 groups of 3 = 15 bubbles twisted + 5 skipped = 20 bubbles total → 5-pointed star.

## Variations

Alberto mentions that this base technique has led to many variations — the fundamental skill of consistent bubble sizing and the twist-skip pattern can be adapted into different shapes and more complex designs.
