## Overview

A comparison guide for the four main types of transparent globe balloons available through Sempertex Europe. Each type has different properties (shape, valve, helium suitability, stuffing ease) that make it better suited to specific design applications. Choosing the right globe for the job avoids wasted time and disappointing results.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Eliminates trial-and-error when selecting transparent balloons
- Prevents common mistakes (e.g. using helium in a balloon that leaks quickly)
- Each globe type has a clear "best use" — knowing this saves money and time
- Product knowledge like this separates professionals from hobbyists

## Detailed Product Profiles

### Glossy Globes

- Come in packs of 5 in three sizes (10", 18", 26").
- Behave like a latex balloon — so while helium technically works, air-fill is the intended use.
- No valve — making them ideal for stuffing (no obstruction when inserting items).
- **Best for:** Air-fill designs where you want to stuff the balloon.

### Crystal Clearz (Anagram)

- Available in 10" and 18–22" sizes.
- Come with a valve — this makes stuffing slightly harder, but you can cut the valve off.
- Helium works but escapes faster than in other globe types.
- Available in a wide range of transparent colours (not just clear) — useful for themed designs where you want colour but still transparency.
- **Best for:** Air-fill designs where you don't need to stuff, or where you want coloured transparency.

### Deco Bubbles

- Available in 20" and 24" sizes.
- No valve — easy to stuff.
- Work equally well with helium and air-fill.
- Available with printed designs — great for themed events.
- **Downside:** Not perfectly spherical — they tend to be slightly flat/oval rather than round.
- **Best for:** Helium designs where you want a printed globe.

### Grabo Globes

- Available in 15" (transparent), 16" (colour fronts), and 19" (transparent).
- No valve — easy to stuff.
- Work excellently with both helium and air-fill.
- **Key differentiator:** Constructed from 4 equal parts (instead of the standard 2 halves), which makes them perfectly round — the most spherical of all transparent globes.
- Available in fully transparent, colour fronts, and solid colours.
- **Best for:** Helium designs where a perfectly round shape matters.

## Quick Decision Guide

| I want to… | Use |
|---|---|
| Make an air-fill design (no stuffing) | Crystal Clearz |
| Make an air-fill design with stuffing | Glossy Globes |
| Make a helium design with a print | Deco Bubbles |
| Make a helium design with a perfect round shape | Grabo Globes |
| Use transparent colour (not just clear) | Crystal Clearz or Grabo Globes (colour fronts) |

---

## Tips

- **Valve = harder to stuff.** If stuffing is important, choose a globe without a valve (Glossy Globes, Deco Bubbles, or Grabo Globes) — or cut the valve off Crystal Clearz
- **Helium in latex-behaving globes leaks faster** — if float time matters, choose Deco Bubbles or Grabo Globes over Glossy Globes
- **Shape matters for premium work** — Grabo Globes' 4-part construction gives the most professional-looking sphere; Deco Bubbles can look slightly flat
- **Crystal Clearz colour range** opens up creative options that other transparent globes don't offer — coloured but still see-through

## Business Applications

### Use cases
- Stuffed balloon gifts (Glossy Globes or Grabo Globes)
- Helium centrepieces (Grabo Globes for shape, Deco Bubbles for prints)
- Themed event decor with transparent colour (Crystal Clearz)
- Confetti-filled designs (any no-valve globe)
- Balloon-in-balloon / nested designs

### Pricing
- Globe balloons command a higher price than standard latex — the transparency adds perceived value
- Stuffed globes are a high-margin product (low material cost, high labour perception)
- Helium globes with prints (Deco Bubbles) work well for off-the-shelf retail or delivery services

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Globe | A transparent (clear or coloured) round balloon — distinct from standard latex rounds |
| Valve | A self-sealing mechanism on some globe types that retains air/helium but obstructs stuffing |
| Stuffing | Inserting items (smaller balloons, confetti, gifts, feathers) inside a transparent globe |
| Air-fill | Inflating with air (via pump) rather than helium — balloon does not float |
| Float time | How long a helium-inflated balloon stays buoyant — varies significantly by balloon type |
| 4-part construction | Grabo Globes' manufacturing method using 4 equal panels instead of 2 halves, creating a more perfect sphere |
| Colour front | A Grabo Globe variant where the front panel is coloured and the rest is transparent |
| Deco Bubble | A brand of transparent globe known for printed designs and dual helium/air-fill suitability |
| Crystal Clearz | Anagram's transparent globe line — available in multiple colours with a valve |
