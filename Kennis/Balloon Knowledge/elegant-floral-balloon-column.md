## Overview

A classic tapered floral balloon column built on a floor-standing pole frame, featuring graduated balloon sizes from base to top, a stuffed Bobo topper, and artificial florals/foliage for an elegant finish. This is a high-value event piece suitable for entrances, stage framing, and centerpiece displays.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Classic, elegant design that works for weddings, corporate events, galas
- Tapered shape creates a professional, polished look
- Foliage and florals add perceived value with minimal cost
- Bobo topper is perfect for vinyl personalisation
- Excellent hire product — florals and frame are reusable
- Higher profit margin when hired vs. sold

## Technique: Building the Tapered Base

### Creating quads (clusters of 4)

1. **Inflate to size** using a sizer. Round the balloons out for a clean shape.
2. **Create duplets.** Cross the balloon over and tie tight.
3. **Combine two duplets into a quad.** Slide one duplet in one direction, lock with the second duplet, push down tight in the middle.
4. **Optional extra lock:** Twist two opposing balloons together for added security.

### Master tip: underinflation technique

When underinflating 12" balloons (e.g., to 6"), **push the latex all the way down onto the nozzle of the inflator** before inflating. This prevents excess uninflated latex collecting at the top of the balloon, which causes faster oxidation and an uneven shape. This makes a significant difference in the final appearance and longevity.

### Tapered base assembly

Work from large to small, bottom to top:
1. **8" quad** at the very bottom (widest)
2. **6" quad** on top of that (medium)
3. **5" quad** on top (smallest)

Each quad slides onto the pole and nests into the one below. Tuck nozzles away for neatness.

## Technique: Building the Top Clusters

### Important difference from base

Top clusters don't have weight above them to hold them straight — they'll sit slightly skewed until the topper is added. This is normal.

### Assembly

1. Add **5" quad** (Coral) — slide onto pole, position in gaps of the base cluster below
2. Add **6" quad** (Fashion Melon) — lock into place, ensure balloons sit between the gaps of the cluster below
3. Push clusters together so they sit snugly

### Pole protection

Before adding the top cluster, **protect the latex from the sharp pole tip:**
- Take a 5" latex balloon (uninflated)
- Open it wide with your fingers
- Stretch it over the top of the pole
- This prevents the metal tip from popping the topper or top cluster balloons

### Top cluster

Add the **8" quad** (Periwinkle) on top, twist into place.

## Technique: Attaching the Topper

### Topper setup
- 36" Sempertex Bobo with a 26" Periwinkle latex inflated inside
- A clear 260 tied to the Bobo's neck as an attachment point
- This Bobo is ideal for vinyl personalisation

### Securing the topper (critical for stability)

1. Place the Bobo on top of the column.
2. Take the 260 attachment and **wrap it around the first (top) cluster.**
3. Do a **crisscross** around the cluster (don't tie — just cross).
4. Pull tight and **continue all the way down to the bottom cluster.**
5. Tie off with **three knots** for security.
6. Test: pull the 260 — it should snap back into place. If excess shows, trim it off.

**This prevents the topper from wobbling** — essential for outdoor setups or busy venues.

## Technique: Adding Organic Texture

Small metallic balloons add visual depth between the main clusters.

1. Inflate a metallic 5" balloon with **one pump only** — keep them small.
2. Twist at the base to keep it tight and round.
3. Trim excess latex and tie tight, leaving a **long attachment tail.**
4. Find an existing balloon nozzle in the cluster and tie the small balloon to it.
5. Repeat — add 3-4 small metallic balloons scattered across the design.
6. **Leave intentional gaps** in your cluster spacing for foliage placement.

## Technique: Adding Foliage

### Why not to glue foliage

1. **Reusability:** If you hire the design out, you get your foliage back when you retrieve the stand.
2. **Latex damage:** Glue pulls on latex, making tears more likely — especially with temperature changes or if someone bumps the display.

### How to attach foliage

**Method 1: Tuck and weave (preferred)**
- Simply tuck foliage stems between the balloons.
- Weave longer pieces (e.g., palm leaves) around the pole and through the clusters.
- The latex naturally grips the foliage and holds it in place.
- Don't push all the way through — just tuck in enough to secure.

**Method 2: 260 wrapping (for permanent/sold pieces)**
- Take an uninflated 260.
- Wrap it around the floral stem and the surrounding latex.
- Do figure-eights at the back to distribute pressure.
- Don't apply too much pressure — you can pop the latex.

### Planning for foliage

- **Leave gaps open intentionally** where you know foliage will go.
- Don't fill every space with balloons — foliage needs room to sit naturally.
- **Add all foliage before flowers** — foliage frames the design, flowers are the accents.

---

## Technique: Adding Flowers

- Open the flower heads and spread petals before inserting.
- If the stem has no sharp point: simply push gently between balloons.
- If the stem has a sharp point: wrap the tip in a small piece of latex (5") first to protect surrounding balloons.
- For sold pieces: secure with a 260 wrapped around the stem and tied to surrounding balloons.

### Costing tip

Always factor florals into your pricing — whether hiring or selling. Hire generates more profit because you reuse the florals and frame.

## Front-Facing vs. Two-Sided

- **Front-facing (entrance/wall):** Only decorate the front.
- **Two-sided (centerpiece/walkway):** Decorate both sides — an undecorated back looks unprofessional.

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Duplet | Two balloons tied together at the neck |
| Quad / Cluster | Four balloons (two duplets) combined into a single group |
| Tapered base | A column base that graduates from large at the bottom to small at the top |
| Sizer | A tool (often a box with holes) used to inflate balloons to a consistent, specific diameter |
| Bobo | A transparent PVC/TPU balloon used for stuffing and personalisation |
| Stuffed bubble | A Bobo with an inflated latex balloon inside for color effect |
| Oxidation | The natural dulling/chalky appearance of latex over time — accelerated by excess uninflated latex |
| Figure-eight | A wrapping technique where a 260 crosses in a figure-8 pattern to distribute tension evenly |
| Underinflation | Inflating a balloon to less than its stated size (e.g., a 12" balloon inflated to 6") |
