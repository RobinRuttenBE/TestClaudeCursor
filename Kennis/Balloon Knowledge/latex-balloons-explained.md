## Modelling Balloons

Modelling balloons are elongated latex balloons used for twisting, sculpting, and decorative detailing. Sempertex uses a numbering system where the first digit = diameter in inches and "60" = length (60 inches / 5 feet when fully inflated).

| Balloon | Diameter | Length (fully inflated) | Primary use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **160** | 1 inch | 60 inches (5 ft) | Fine detail work, advanced twisting, delicate accents |
| **260** | 2 inches | 60 inches (5 ft) | The standard twisting balloon — dogs, flowers, characters, garland detailing, organic accents |
| **360** | 3 inches | 60 inches (5 ft) | Larger sculpting, character bodies/heads, creative shapes (e.g., lipstick in a makeup bouquet) |

### Key notes
- The **260 is the most popular** modelling balloon and the default choice for decorators.
- The **160** is used more in the dedicated twisting/entertainment world — less common for decorators but valuable for fine detail.
- The **360** is versatile for creative applications — anywhere you need a thicker modelling shape (body parts for characters, structural elements, novelty shapes like lipstick tubes).
- Modelling balloons are rarely fully inflated — underinflation gives better workability and allows room for twisting without popping.

## Round Balloons

Round balloons are the core product for decoration. Sempertex uses the prefix **R** (for Round) followed by the size in inches.

| Balloon | Size | Typical inflation | Primary use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R5** | 5 inch | Fully inflated or slightly under | Garland depth and detail, small accents, organic texture, cluster fillers |
| **R9** | 9 inch | Fully inflated | Mid-size garland depth, bases — fills the gap between 5" and 12" |
| **R10** | 10 inch | Fully inflated | Similar to R9 — primarily European market |
| **R12** | 12 inch | Fully inflated or underinflated to various sizes | The workhorse — garlands, bases, bouquets, columns, arches, general decoration |
| **R18** | 18 inch | Typically underinflated to ~16" for round shape | Larger garland features, statement pieces, organic installations |
| **R24** | 24 inch | Typically inflated to ~22" for round shape | Large venue garlands, gender reveals, helium displays with tassels |
| **R36** | 36 inch | Typically inflated to 30–32" | Giant statement pieces, event centrepieces, helium displays, arch anchors, content-day impact pieces |

### Key notes

#### Most popular sizes
- **R5 and R12 are the two most-used round balloons.** These cover the vast majority of everyday decoration work — garlands, bases, bouquets.
- The R9 fills a useful middle ground between R5 and R12 and creates better garland depth when all three sizes are used together.

#### The underinflation principle
- **Always underinflate slightly** — this gives stronger balloons with better longevity and a rounder, more professional shape.
- For larger balloons (R18+), underinflation is especially important to maintain a round shape rather than the elongated "traditional" balloon shape.

#### The double-inflation technique (for round shape on larger balloons)
- Inflate the balloon fully, then **deflate it** so the latex stretches.
- Re-inflate to the desired size — the pre-stretched latex now holds a **rounder shape** at full size.
- This is particularly popular for organic installations where round shapes are preferred over pear shapes.

#### R12 underinflation note
- When underinflating a 12" to a smaller size (e.g., 7"), an **R9 inflated to its natural size gives a better color density** and rounder shape than a 12" squeezed down. This is a key reason the R9 is valued by experienced decorators.

#### Large balloons (R24, R36)
- R24 is excellent for **gender reveals** and helium displays — the larger volume provides greater lifting capacity for tassels and accessories.
- R36 creates instant visual impact — most people never see balloons this size in everyday life, which makes them inherently impressive.
- R36 inflated to 30–32" can be used as **cost-effective alternatives to expensive metallic/mirror orbs** when the right color is chosen.
- Large balloons are surprisingly durable when underinflated — they won't pop easily and can withstand handling.

## Link-O-Loons

Link-O-Loons are Sempertex's linking balloons — they have small protruding tails at both ends (instead of just a nozzle) that allow balloons to be tied together in chains, walls, and structures.

| Balloon | Size | Primary use |
|---|---|---|
| **Link-O-Loon 6"** | 6 inch | Chains for bouquets, garland accents, bracelet-style decorative chains, small linking details |
| **Link-O-Loon 12"** | 12 inch | Balloon walls, large-scale linked structures, backdrops |
| **Link-O-Loon Large** | Oversized elongated | Large structural builds — balloon dogs, gingerbread houses, cubes, architectural pieces |

### Key notes
- **6" Link-O-Loons** underinflated slightly and linked together create chain effects — used as decorative additions to bouquets and garlands (described as similar to friendship bracelet chains).
- **12" Link-O-Loons** are the go-to for **balloon walls** — a linked wall structure that can then be made organic with additional balloons layered on top. This uses significantly fewer balloons than building a fully organic wall from scratch.
- **Large Link-O-Loons** are used for architectural/structural balloon builds — giant balloon dogs, novelty structures, display pieces.
- Denise (Sempertex Europe Brand Ambassador) is specifically noted as an expert in Link-O-Loon techniques — she created a fully functional balloon sofa at a trade expo that could seat five people.

### Content note
Link-O-Loon walls as a base for organic designs is a strong educational content topic — it's a technique that saves material and time while achieving the same visual result.

- A physical swatch book containing samples of every Sempertex color.

## The 8 Sempertex Textures

### 1. Fashion
- **The standard balloon texture** — solid, opaque color.
- Contains core staples: white, black, yellows, and the full primary/secondary color spectrum.
- This is where most everyday colors live — if a decorator needs "a red balloon" or "a blue balloon," it's almost certainly Fashion.
- Notable colors: White Sand, Eucalyptus, Purple Orchid.

### 2. Pastel Dusk
- A newer range with **earthy, deep pastel tones**.
- 5 colors in the range.
- Sits between Fashion and Pastel Matte — more depth and warmth than standard pastels.
- Currently very on-trend — the muted, earthy aesthetic is popular across events and interiors.
- Pairs beautifully with White Sand and other neutral tones.

### 3. Neon
- **High-intensity fluorescent colors.**
- Excellent color density — not see-through, which distinguishes Sempertex neons from cheaper alternatives that can look translucent and uneven.
- The latex itself has a distinctive feel/thickness.
- Primary use: disco themes, UV events, children's parties, festival decor, attention-grabbing accent pieces.

### 4. Pearl
- **Pearlised/iridescent finish** — slightly see-through with a shimmer.
- Ideal for **double-stuffing**: place a colored balloon inside a Pearl White balloon to create a tinted pearlescent effect.
- Very popular for **weddings** — the subtle shimmer adds elegance without being overly metallic.
- The white Pearl is the standout seller in this range.

### 5. Metallic
- Similar shimmer to Pearl but with **brighter, more saturated base colors**.
- Pearl = pastel-toned shimmer. Metallic = fashion/primary-toned shimmer.
- Colors lean toward classic rainbow primaries — brighter and more vivid than Pearl.

### 6. Pastel Matte
- **One of the most popular textures overall.**
- Soft, flat, opaque finish — no shimmer, no shine.
- The quintessential children's party balloon aesthetic — Peppa Pig colors, baby showers, summery themes.
- Notable colors: Fashion Melon (standout), Malibu Peach (newer addition).
- Works for: baby showers, children's birthdays, spring/summer events, pastel-themed weddings.

### 7. Silk
- **A newer range** with 7 colors.
- Described as a cross between Reflex and Pastel — **pastel tones with a chrome-like density**.
- Very thick color — completely opaque, no light passes through.
- Creates a luxurious, premium feel without the full mirror effect of Reflex.
- Unique texture that doesn't exist in competitor ranges — a differentiator.

### 8. Reflex
- **Chrome/mirror finish** — the premium texture.
- Completely opaque — looks and feels like polished metal.
- The most visually striking texture in the range.
- Popular colors: Champagne, Silver, Gold (the top 3 sellers in Reflex).
- Notable new addition: **Aurora Green** — a highly anticipated color launch.
- Reflex Gold was one of the three most in-demand colors during COVID supply shortages.
- These are premium-priced balloons that justify premium pricing in finished work.
