## Overview

A "glass balloon" is a latex balloon inflated inside a transparent plastic balloon (Bobo or Tea Balloon), creating a glossy, colored, premium-looking balloon. This is one of the most fundamental techniques in modern balloon decoration — it's the base for vinyl personalisation, bow additions, column toppers, and standalone gift balloons.

**Key selling points for balloon professionals:**
- Transforms a standard latex balloon into a premium product
- Perfect base for vinyl personalisation (text adheres better to smooth plastic surface)
- Significantly longer lasting than latex alone
- High perceived value — looks expensive and "classy"
- Easy upsell from standard latex or foil balloons
- Works with multiple transparent balloon types (Bobos, Tea Balloons, Globes)

## Technique: Making a Glass Balloon

### Materials
- 1x transparent balloon (Bobo, Tea Balloon, or Globe)
- 1x Sempertex latex balloon (sized to fit — see size guide below)
- Balloon inflator
- Optional: balloon stick for insertion

### Step 1: Prepare the latex

Roll or fold the latex balloon into a narrow pencil shape so it can be inserted through the neck of the transparent balloon.

### Step 2: Insert the latex

- Open the neck of the transparent balloon.
- Slowly insert the rolled latex balloon through the neck.
- A balloon stick can help push it in if the neck is tight.

### Step 3: Remove all trapped air (critical)

- Before inflating, **squeeze out all air** from between the latex and the transparent balloon.
- Scrunch the transparent balloon around the latex to push air out.
- This is the most important step — any trapped air creates visible wrinkles and ruins the "glass" effect.

### Step 4: Inflate

- Place the latex nozzle onto the inflator.
- **Hold both the latex nozzle and the transparent balloon neck together** on the inflator — this prevents air from sneaking in between the two layers.
- Inflate slowly. Don't go too fast — the latex can pop if it catches on the inside of the transparent balloon.
- The goal is a **vacuum effect** where the latex presses flush against the transparent balloon with no air gap.

### Step 5: Check for wrinkles

- Especially with Tea Balloons: check for wrinkles during inflation.
- If you see air between the layers or wrinkles forming: **deflate, remove all air again, and re-inflate.** It's worth redoing to get a seamless result.

### Step 6: Tie off

- Pull the latex nozzle down sharply.
- Wrap around two fingers and tie tight.
- **Include the transparent balloon in the knot** — tie both together so the latex can't slip out.

## Size Guide

| Transparent balloon | Latex inside | Helium float? |
|---|---|---|
| 18" Bobo | 18" latex | Yes (light) |
| 24" Bobo | 24" latex | Yes |
| 36" Bobo | 24" latex | Yes |
| 20" Tea Balloon | 18" latex | No — too heavy |
| 24" Tea Balloon | 24" latex | Marginal |

**Note:** 20" Tea Balloons will not float with helium due to the combined weight of the tea balloon material and the latex inside. Use air-filled on a stick or as a topper.

---

## Tips

- **Bobo balloons need pre-stretching** before use — Tea Balloons and Globes do not
- **Inflate slowly** — rushing causes the inner balloon to catch and pop
- **The seamless look is everything** — if there's visible air between layers, redo it
- Tea Balloons take longer to inflate due to thicker material — be patient
- For the 36" Bobo, a 24" latex inside is sufficient (not 36")

## Applications & Add-ons

- **Vinyl lettering** — the smooth plastic surface is ideal for vinyl (see: vinyl application technique)
- **Bows** — stick-on fabric or balloon bows adhere beautifully to the plastic surface
- **Column toppers** — glass balloons make premium toppers for balloon columns
- **Gift balloons** — standalone glass balloons with personalisation = high-value gift product
- **Stuffed with confetti** — add confetti between the layers before inflating for a different look

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Glass balloon | A latex balloon inflated inside a transparent balloon, creating a glossy premium look |
| Stuffed bubble | Alternative name for a glass balloon |
| Bobo | A transparent PVC/TPU balloon — the most common type used for glass balloons |
| Tea balloon | A thicker, more durable transparent balloon — doesn't need pre-stretching |
| Globe | A transparent balloon similar to Tea Balloons — brand-specific term |
| Pre-stretching | Manually stretching a Bobo before use to make it more pliable and easier to work with |
| Vacuum effect | The desired result where the latex presses flush against the transparent balloon with no air gap |
| Nozzle | The neck/opening of a balloon used for inflation and tying |
