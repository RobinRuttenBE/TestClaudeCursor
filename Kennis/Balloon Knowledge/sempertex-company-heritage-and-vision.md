## Company Heritage

### Family business — three generations

- Sempertex was founded in **1938** by Leo's grandfather.
- The company has been **family-owned and family-run** since its founding.
- **First generation:** Leo's grandfather (founder).
- **Second generation:** Leo's uncles ran the business.
- **Third generation:** Leo joined in **2013** and is now CEO — approximately 11 years in the business at time of interview.

### Leo's path through the company

Leo didn't step straight into the CEO role — he worked across multiple functions:

1. **SAP implementation** — joined during the rollout of enterprise management software.
2. **Strategy Director** — part of the strategy team.
3. **Production Manager** — hands-on operations experience.
4. **New facility project lead** — designed, built, and negotiated the new production facility.
5. **CEO / Director General** — current role.

**Content value:** This progression shows a leader who understands every part of the business — from factory floor to boardroom. It's authentic, not inherited-title corporate. This is strong storytelling material.

## Manufacturing

### In-house engineering

A fact that most people in the industry don't know: **Sempertex designs and manufactures its own production machinery internally.** The production lines are engineered by Sempertex's own engineering team, not bought off-the-shelf from third-party equipment suppliers.

**Why this matters:**
- It means Sempertex can continuously improve its manufacturing process.
- Quality and quality **consistency** are directly controlled — not dependent on external machinery limitations.
- It's a genuine competitive moat — competitors using standard industry equipment can't match the same level of process control.
- It's a powerful differentiator in content: "The company that builds the machines that build the balloons."

### New production facility

- Sempertex has built a **new production facility** designed for the next **20 years** of growth.
- The site is **43,000 square metres** (just over 4 hectares).
- Currently only **25% of the land is developed** — the remaining 75% is reserved for future expansion.
- This signals long-term commitment to the balloon industry — Sempertex is investing in decades of growth, not short-term gains.

**Content value:** This counters any narrative that the balloon industry is declining. The largest manufacturer is building a facility for 20+ years of future growth.

## Sustainability

### The core challenge

The biggest environmental concern in the balloon industry is **consumer perception:**

1. **Many people still think balloons are plastic.** They are not — balloons are made from **latex, which is the sap of a rubber tree.** It is an organic, natural product.
2. **Post-use concern:** Even when people understand balloons are latex, they want to know what happens after the event. "What happens to the balloon afterwards?" is the question the industry must answer.

### Sempertex's sustainability initiative: recycled balloon products

Sempertex is developing the **capacity to recycle used balloons into other products:**

- **Durr** — the first product line: **pet/dog toys made from recycled balloons.**
- This is not a one-off product — Sempertex is building the **manufacturing capacity** to produce multiple recycled-balloon products.
- Future recycled products in development include: **shoe soles, rubber bands**, and any other rubber-based product that can be made from recycled latex.
- The vision: used balloons become raw material for a secondary product line, creating a circular economy within the business.

**Content value:**
- This is not greenwashing — it's a concrete product (Durr) already launched, with manufacturing capacity being built for more.
- "We're not just talking about sustainability — we're building the machines to do it" is a powerful message.
- This benefits the entire industry, not just Sempertex — Leo explicitly states this.
- Balloon professionals can reference this when clients raise environmental concerns.

### Key messaging points for sustainability content

1. **Balloons are NOT plastic** — they are natural latex from rubber trees.
2. **Sempertex is investing in recycling infrastructure** — not just promises, but manufacturing capability.
3. **Recycled balloon products already exist** — Durr pet toys are launched and available.
4. **The vision is circular** — used balloons become raw material for new products.
5. **This benefits the whole industry** — it's not a Sempertex-only initiative in spirit; it raises the bar for everyone.

## Industry Challenges (CEO Perspective)

Leo identifies three key challenges facing the balloon industry:

### 1. Environmental perception
- Overcoming the "balloons = plastic" misconception.
- Answering the post-use question ("What happens to balloons after the event?").
- Proactively investing in solutions (recycling) rather than waiting for regulation to force change.

### 2. Regulatory environment
- The **European Union** is updating regulations that will place new requirements on all balloon manufacturers.
- These relate to product safety and compliance for products released into the EU market.
- Sempertex is proactively staying ahead of these requirements.

**Content value:** This positions Sempertex (and by extension STX EU) as a compliant, responsible manufacturer — important for B2B buyers who need to trust their supply chain.

### 3. Education for new generations
- New generations of decorators and consumers are entering the industry.
- The industry must provide **high-quality education** so newcomers understand:
  - How the industry works
  - That balloon decoration is a viable, professional livelihood
  - Best practices for quality, sustainability, and business

**Content value:** This directly supports STX EU's educational content strategy — it's not just a marketing tactic, it's the CEO's stated priority for the company's future.
